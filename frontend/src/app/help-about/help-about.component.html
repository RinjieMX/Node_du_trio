How Anki Works

Introduction

Anki is a spaced repetition application that uses flashcards to facilitate learning and memorization.

Basic Principle

Anki employs the spaced repetition algorithm to schedule card reviews at optimal times, maximizing long-term retention.

Creating Cards

Users can create cards containing questions and answers. These cards are then reviewed based on the user's mastery level.

  • Questions on the front, answers on the back.
  • Use of media (images, audio, video) to enhance cards.

Daily Review

Anki offers a daily review of cards, automatically adjusting the repetition interval based on the user's performance.
